ML for Software Engineering

I'm a Ph.D. student advised by Dr. Tim Menzies, working on making machine learning and optimization more practical for software engineering. My research focuses on fuzzing, multi-objective optimization, and data-efficient methods.

Data-Centric Fuzzing for JavaScript Engines

with Tim Menzies | 2025

We developed a machine learning-guided fuzzing framework that learns from crash patterns to discover vulnerabilities in JavaScript engines. Our approach combines feature extraction from real crashes with SHAP-based mutation strategies, achieving 85%+ precision. This work discovered a previously unknown V8 crash.

BINGO: Simple Optimizers for SE

with Tim Menzies | 2025

We discovered that multi-objective optimization problems in software engineering collapse to just 1-2% of their theoretical search space. This "BINGO effect" means simple optimizers can reach >90% of optimal performance with ≤50 evaluations, drastically reducing computational cost.

MOOT: Multi-Objective Optimization Tasks

with Tim Menzies, Tao Chen, Yulong Ye, and others | 2025

Created a standardized benchmark of 120+ real-world multi-objective optimization tasks spanning software configuration, cloud tuning, and process modeling. MOOT enables reproducible research and fair comparison of optimization methods.
